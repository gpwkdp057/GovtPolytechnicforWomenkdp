history of college

Government Polytechnic for Women, Kadapa was established in 1985. The college is approved by the All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE) and the Pharmacy Council of India (PCI). It is an affiliated college of the State Board of Technical Education and Training, Andhra Pradesh. The college provides diploma courses in the fields of Engineering and Architecture, and Pharmacy streams to students. Government Polytechnic for Women, Kadapa courses cover the Diploma programmes in five domains. The duration of the diploma courses is three years for Engineering programmes and two years for the Pharmacy programme. Government Polytechnic for Women, Kadapa admissions to the diploma courses in the Engineering stream are offered based on the “Polytechnic Common Entrance Test (AP PolyCET)” examination rank. Government Polytechnic for Women, Kadapa facilities include library facilities, sports facilities, laboratory facilities and many more facilities..

Vision and Mission

INSTITUTION VISION :

Transforming the young budding professionals, globally competitive in the service and entrepreneur sector through excellence in the technical education, to contribute social enrichment. Building a legacy of excellence is our intent and positioning the institute as a compelling choice for potential students. In the current dynamic and highly fluid environment of technical education, our focus is to impart industry ready technocrats who can adapt to every challenge of the industry.

INSTITUTION MISSION :

Incorporating best possible innovative instructional techniques. Providing a strong theoretical foundation, practical engineering skills and promoting research activities. Developing leadership qualities, experience in interpersonal communication and teamwork. Inculcating professional conduct, ethics, critical thinking, trouble shooting and the attitude of life-long learning.
